Overview

The tool was built to evaluate design trade-offs for SMR fuel rod configurations using a simplified but physics-informed model.

Design variables

Rod length, rod diameter, rod count, fuel type, and structural material selections.

Physics link

Geometry drives fuel mass, burnup drives total available energy, and power mode determines how quickly that fuel is used.

Economic outputs

Core procurement cost, lifecycle fuel-cycle cost, rod change interval, and normalized cost per MWh.

Power modes

  • Fixed reactor power: the app sizes the rod count based on the selected core thermal power and an assumed linear heat rate for the reactor type.
  • Power scales with rod count: the app computes core thermal power from rod count, rod length, and the assumed linear heat rate.

Burnup calculation

Burnup is used to convert heavy metal mass into total thermal energy before refueling.

E_th (kWh) = Burnup (GWd/tHM) × Fuel mass (tHM) × 24 × 1,000,000

Key metrics

The app reports metrics that are useful for fair comparison across reactor designs and fuel types.

Core procurement cost

Estimated cost of front-end fuel services, fabrication process cost, and structural hardware for the modeled reactor core.

Rod change interval

Estimated fuel lifetime before replacement, based on burnup, fuel mass, power, and capacity factor.

Lifecycle $/MWh

Fuel-cycle cost normalized by total energy produced. This is the best metric for comparing options with different energy densities.
