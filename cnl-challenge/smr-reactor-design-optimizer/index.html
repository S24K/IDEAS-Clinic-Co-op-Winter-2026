Optimize SMR fuel rod design with physics-informed cost modeling.

This project is an interactive SMR fuel rod optimization tool that combines simplified reactor physics, burnup-based lifetime estimation, and fuel-cycle cost modeling to compare design trade-offs across fuel types, materials, and operating modes.

What the app does

Users configure reactor type, rod geometry, fuel, structural materials, and operating assumptions. The application then estimates rod lifetime, core procurement cost, lifecycle fuel-cycle cost, and normalized economics such as $/MWh.

Project Overview

The tool was built to support reactor design exploration for small modular reactors by linking geometry, materials, operating assumptions, and fuel-cycle economics in one interactive workflow.

Design Inputs

Users choose reactor type, rod length, rod diameter, rod count, fuel type, and structural materials.

Physics Model

Fuel mass is estimated from rod geometry, then burnup is used to approximate total thermal energy and rod lifetime.

Economic Outputs

The app reports procurement cost, lifecycle fuel-cycle cost, annual cost proxies, and normalized $/MWh metrics.

How It Works

The application follows a simple engineering pipeline from geometry to energy to economics.

1
Set geometry
Rod length, diameter, rod count
2
Select materials
UO₂, MOX, TRISO and hardware materials
3
Estimate fuel mass
Heavy metal mass derived from calibrated geometry
4
Compute energy
Burnup × fuel mass → total thermal energy
5
Evaluate cost
Front-end + fabrication + hardware + backend

Power modes

  • Fixed reactor power: given core thermal power, the app sizes rod count using an assumed linear heat rate for the chosen SMR type.
  • Power scales with rod count: core thermal power is computed from rod count, rod length, and an assumed linear heat rate.

Burnup-based lifetime

The model converts burnup from GWd/tHM into total thermal energy. That energy is then divided by annual thermal production to estimate the rod replacement interval.

E_th (kWh) = Burnup (GWd/tHM) × Fuel mass (tHM) × 24 × 1,000,000

Reported Metrics

The application focuses on metrics that are useful for comparing fuel designs fairly.

Core procurement cost

Estimated cost of front-end fuel services, fabrication process cost, and structural hardware for the modeled core.

Rod change interval

Estimated fuel lifetime before replacement, based on burnup, fuel mass, power, and capacity factor.

Lifecycle $/MWh

Fuel-cycle cost normalized by energy produced. This is the best metric for comparing fuel options with different energy densities.
